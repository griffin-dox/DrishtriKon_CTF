What is Capture The Flag?

Capture The Flag (CTF) is a special kind of cybersecurity competition designed to challenge participants to solve computer security problems and/or capture and defend computer systems. These competitions typically involve:

  • Finding hidden "flags" (specific strings) through solving challenges
  • Competing against other players or teams
  • Learning and applying various cybersecurity skills
  • Exploring different categories like web exploitation, cryptography, reverse engineering, and more

Challenge Categories

Web

Challenges focusing on web application vulnerabilities, including XSS, CSRF, SQL injection, and authentication bypasses.

Cryptography

Challenges involving encryption, decryption, hashing, and breaking or implementing cryptographic algorithms.

Forensics

Challenges involving file analysis, steganography, memory analysis, and network packet inspection.

Reverse Engineering

Challenges requiring analysis of binaries, understanding program behavior without source code.

Pwnable

Challenges focusing on binary exploitation, such as buffer overflows, use-after-free, and format string vulnerabilities.

Miscellaneous

Variety of challenges that don't fit in other categories, often involving general problem-solving skills.

OSINT

Open Source Intelligence challenges that involve gathering information from publicly available sources.

Lab Challenges

Practice challenges available at any time, designed for learning and skill development outside of competitions.
